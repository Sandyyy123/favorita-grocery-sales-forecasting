Motivation

Demand planning at a national grocery chain is a hierarchical, exogenously driven, partly intermittent forecasting problem. Edeka, Rewe, Lidl, dm, and Rossmann run the same problem at SKU-store-day granularity in DACH. The Corporacion Favorita dataset is the most realistic public proxy for that production stack outside the M5 competition itself.

Why this dataset.
  • Hierarchical: country, state, city, store, family.
  • Holiday calendar with national, regional, and local resolution.
  • Macroeconomic exogenous driver (oil price, Ecuador-specific).
  • April 2016 Pedernales earthquake creates a real-world disruption to handle.
  • Mix of high-volume families (GROCERY I, BEVERAGES) and intermittent families (BOOKS, BABY CARE).

Hierarchy and intermittency

  • 54 stores across 22 cities and 16 states, 5 store types (A-E), 17 clusters.
  • 33 product families; 1,782 active store-family series.
  • Three families dominate volume: GROCERY I, BEVERAGES, PRODUCE.
  • Several families are intermittent (Croston regime): BOOKS, BABY CARE, HARDWARE, PET SUPPLIES with greater than 50% zero-sales rows.

Seasonality

  • Weekly seasonality with weekend uplift.
  • Annual seasonality dominated by December peak; secondary peaks in May (Mother's Day) and August (back-to-school).
  • April 2016 earthquake creates a multi-week disruption visible in the country-level total.

Methods

Validation protocol

Single time-based holdout: last 16 days of train (2017-07-31 to 2017-08-15) serve as in-sample validation, mirroring the Kaggle public-test window. Rolling-origin cross-validation is deferred to Phase 2.

Metrics

  • RMSLE for point forecasts (Kaggle metric; predictions clipped at 0).
  • Pinball loss at tau in {0.1, 0.5, 0.9} for probabilistic forecasts (strictly proper scoring rule).

Baseline: Per-Series Prophet

Implementation in src/model_baseline.py. Multiplicative seasonality, changepoint_prior_scale 0.05, weekly + yearly seasonality enabled. Effective-holiday DataFrame built by filtering transferred=False and folding in Transfer-typed rows. Oil price added via add_regressor. SARIMA(1,1,1)(1,1,1,7) fallback if Prophet is unavailable.

for store_nbr, family in series_keys:
    s = train[(train.store_nbr == store_nbr) & (train.family == family)]
    model = Prophet(weekly=True, yearly=True, holidays=eff,
                    seasonality_mode='multiplicative')
    model.add_regressor('dcoilwtico')

Advanced: Panel LightGBM with Engineered Features

Implementation in src/model_advanced.py. Single LightGBM regressor trained on the full panel of 1,782 series, with 27 engineered features and three quantile heads for prediction intervals.

Feature menu (27 columns)

  • Lag features: sales_lag_{1, 7, 14, 28} per (store_nbr, family).
  • Rolling stats: sales_roll_{7, 28}_mean and sales_roll_{7, 28}_std, shifted by 1 day to avoid leak.
  • Calendar: dayofweek, day, month, weekofyear, is_weekend, is_month_end, is_month_start.
  • Holidays: national_holiday, regional_holiday, local_holiday (booleans).
  • Promotions: onpromotion (raw count) plus onpromotion_lag_7.
  • Macro: dcoilwtico (forward-filled WTI).
  • Earthquake flag: is_post_quake (16 April 2016 to 15 May 2016).
  • Categoricals: store_nbr, family, city, state, type, cluster (LightGBM native).

Training

  • Target transform: log1p(sales) to align loss with RMSLE.
  • Settings: num_leaves=127, learning_rate=0.05, feature_fraction=0.8, bagging_fraction=0.8, min_data_in_leaf=100.
  • Up to 3,000 boosting rounds with early-stopping patience 100.
  • Three additional fits with objective='quantile' and alpha in {0.1, 0.5, 0.9}.

Discussion

Known limitations of the Phase 1 scaffold

  1. No hierarchical reconciliation. Bottom-level forecasts do not aggregate consistently up to country / state / family totals. MinT reconciliation [Athanasopoulos 2009] is a Phase 2 deliverable.
  2. Intermittent families fitted with the same regressor. BOOKS, BABY CARE, HARDWARE, PET SUPPLIES need a Croston / TSB head [Croston 1972, Kim 2016].
  3. Locale-aware holidays not joined per store. Regional holidays apply nationally; matching by store.city is a one-day Phase 2 fix.
  4. Quantile heads only at three quantiles. Full-distribution forecasts via DeepAR or NBEATSx [Salinas 2020, Olivares 2023] would expose the joint dependence structure.
  5. Single time-based holdout. Rolling-origin cross-validation [Bergmeir 2018] is the standard for Phase 2.

Connection to DACH demand planning

The Favorita scaffold transfers to a DACH grocery or pharmacy chain with three substitutions: replace holidays_events.csv with the Bundesland-level public holiday and Schulferien calendar; replace oil.csv with a relevant macro driver (consumer-price index or weather); re-fit. The validation protocol (16-day in-sample holdout, RMSLE) and the metric carry over without modification. Lidl and Edeka publicly describe a similar feature menu (lag, rolling, calendar, holidays, promotions, weather) plus a separate intermittent-demand head for slow movers.

Why LightGBM, not a deep transformer

M5 competition evidence is unambiguous on this point. Tabular gradient boosting beat tuned deep tabular models at both the point-accuracy and uncertainty tracks [Makridakis 2022], at a fraction of the training cost. A panel LightGBM fit on Favorita completes in roughly 10-30 minutes on a single CPU; a TFT or DeepAR fit at equivalent capacity takes several hours on GPU. The Phase 2 plan adds these models for the families where LightGBM most clearly underperforms, rather than swapping the whole stack.

Conclusion

Phase 1 scaffold complete. Two reproducible modelling pipelines (Prophet per-series, LightGBM panel with quantile heads), one EDA notebook, one IMRaD manuscript with 26 verified academic references, and this self-contained presentation. Numerical results land in deliverables/ after main-session execution. Phase 2 priorities, in order: hierarchical reconciliation; intermittent-demand head; locale-aware holidays; rolling-origin cross-validation; FFORMA ensembling; deep-learning add-ons (TFT, DeepAR, NBEATSx).
